The Problem with Pros/Cons Lists

Pros/cons lists treat every factor as equally important. "Has free parking" counts the same as "doubles my salary." You end up counting items instead of weighing them — and the option with more minor perks wins over the one with fewer but life-changing advantages.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is AHP (Analytic Hierarchy Process)?

AHP is a mathematical framework for multi-criteria decision analysis developed by Thomas Saaty in the 1970s. It is used by NASA, Boeing, the World Bank, and Fortune 500 companies. Instead of ranking options directly, you compare criteria and alternatives in pairs on a 1-to-9 scale. The math (eigenvector calculation) then produces precise priority weights. AHP includes a consistency ratio check that flags when your judgments contradict each other.

What is Devil's Advocate in Decisio?

Devil's Advocate is an AI feature that challenges your top-ranked choice before you commit. It surfaces specific risks, blind spots, and counterarguments based on your actual criteria, options, and scores — not generic warnings. It helps you stress-test your decision and catch things you might have missed.

What is the Decision Playbook?

The Playbook captures your decision-making wisdom over time. Save Recipes (strategies and approaches that worked well) and Principles (lessons learned from past decisions). Each entry can link back to the original decision. Over time, your Playbook becomes a personal operating manual for making better choices.
